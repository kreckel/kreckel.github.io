Great Debates in Galaxy Evolution

Lecturer: Kathryn Kreckel (ARI)
Term: Summer semester, 2023
Dates: 19 April 2023 through 19 July 2023
Times: Weekly: Wednesdays 09:15 – 11:00 am
Location: Mönchhofstr. 12-14 / ARI 1st floor seminar room
Maximum number of students: 12

This is a Master Seminar on 'Great Debates in Galaxy Evolution'.Each student will choose a topic from a list of proposed topics featuring a 'great debate' around an open question (and possible solutions) in the field of galaxy evolution. Each of the students will prepare a 45 minute talk on the chosen topic (with slides and in English). After the talk, the class will participate in a scientific discussion and we will all provide constructive feedback in relation to both the style and content of the presentation.


Topics List: (pdf)
  • (Historical) Shapley–Curtis Debate
  • Missing satellite problem
  • Cusp-core problem
  • G dwarf problem
  • “Impossibly early galaxy” problem
  • Missing baryon problem
  • Cooling catastrophe
  • What defines a galaxy?
  • Co-evolution (or not) of galaxies and SMBHs
  • Angular momentum problem
  • Satellite alignment problem
  • How do galaxies get their gas?
  • too-big-to-fail problem
